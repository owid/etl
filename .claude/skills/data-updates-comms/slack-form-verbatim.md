# Slack form — verbatim text

The exact prompt and example strings shown in the #data-updates-comms Slack form's "Message about new data update" dialog. Used by the `data-updates-comms` skill to render the draft file with prompts that match the form character-for-character. Do **not** edit unless the form itself changes.

---

1. **Prompt:** What dataset(s) did you update?
   **Example:** E.g.: UN World Population Prospects.

2. **Prompt:** When was this data released? When is the next scheduled release / our plan for next update?
   **Example:** E.g.: released: Jul 2024; next: Jul 2027, and we'll update that month.

3. **Prompt:** Who is the data source(s)? Is there anything our users should know about them?
   **Example:** E.g.: Maintained by researchers at the University of California and the Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research.

4. **Prompt:** What's the coverage of the data in terms of years and countries/regions?
   **Example:** E.g.: Covers the years 1991–2024, global total only.

5. **Prompt:** How many charts did this update affect?
   **Example:** Was this a small update affecting a handful of charts, or a massive one affecting tens or even hundreds?

6. **Prompt:** What does this dataset help our users understand about the world, and why is it important they know that?
   **Example:** In other words: Why do we have this data on OWID at all? What is unique about this dataset compared to similar ones?

7. **Prompt:** Any important caveats or pitfalls in interpretation that users should know about this data? (optional)
   **Example:** E.g.: note that saying "Country X is more productive than Country Y" is often taken as "people in Country X work harder".

8. **Prompt:** Anything interesting to note about this update, including what you had to do? Anything else you'd like to add? (optional)
   **Example:** E.g.: you worked with the data provider to improve the data somehow.

9. **Prompt:** Add 1–3 chart views we might use in the public announcement
   **Example:** Pick chart views that represent the whole dataset, rather than, e.g., something very specific about a single country.

10. **Prompt:** Link to the updated charts as a search result (not a chart collection anymore). Ask Charlie if you need help with this. (optional)
    **Example:** E.g.: https://ourworldindata.org/search?datasetProducts=World%20Development%20Indicators.
